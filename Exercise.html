Exploring Geographic Information Systems (GIS)

Data, Maps, and Analysis at Your Fingertips

Did you know? GIS is used to analyze spatial and geographic data to visualize patterns, relationships, and trends.

Key GIS Concepts

Here are some key components of GIS:

  • Spatial Data (e.g., coordinates, locations)
  • Remote Sensing (satellite imagery)
  • Georeferencing (aligning maps with real-world coordinates)
  • Vector and Raster Data (shapes and grids)
  • Analysis (e.g., proximity, overlay, interpolation)
